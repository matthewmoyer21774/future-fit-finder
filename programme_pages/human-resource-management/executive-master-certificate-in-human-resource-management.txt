--- DESCRIPTION ---
Organisations are increasingly looking to their HR colleagues to act as strategic business partners – creating impact and value and actively driving success and growth. 

The Executive Master Certificate in Human Resource Management is a 12-day programme that empowers you to become this strategic partner. You learn new skills and put them into practice in a hands-on, strategic project for your own organisation. When you successfully complete the programme, you’ll be ready to take the leap and drive positive change for your business.

## Module 1: Strategic HRM
The programme comprises five core modules – and each one focuses on a key aspect of human resource management. Through each one, you’ll gain the understanding and approaches you need to make impactful, strategic decisions. 

This world-class HRM programme also gives you an opportunity to gain immediate ROI for your organisation – with an in-company project. You’ll use it to tackle a pressing HR challenge in your organisation – all with the dedicated support of our expert faculty.

Module 1: Strategic HRM 
Distinguish the dynamic relationship between organisational behaviour, organisational design and HRM
Analyse the current dominant HR models and trains of thought in HR
Understand the dynamics of the labour market
Critically reflect upon the way your HR department in organised in your organisation
Evaluate the power and limitations of evidence-based HR   
Module 2: Strategic Talent and Career Management 
Analyse and evaluate your current recruitment and selection policies
Illustrate how employer branding could be applied in your organisation to develop a concrete employer value proposition
Understand the link between training and development and the HR strategy
Evaluate your own self-management triangle when making career choices
Apply the political metaphor on career systems by recognising dominant coalition dynamics 
Module 3: Strategic Competence and Performance Management 
Compose competency profiles based on jobs and people in the organisation
Assess your organisation’s approach to managing employee performance in the light of new trends
Design a performance management approach fitting organisational maturity and culture
Customise performance management based on the contribution and growth potential of individual employees
Module 4: Strategic Reward Management
Develop strategically inspired reward techniques and implement them in your company
Become familiar with practical concepts and tools to apply in remuneration schemes
Discover how to position rewards, as well as strategic embedding within the context of the organisation
Gain insight into the latest concepts in reward management
Module 5: Strategic Challenges for HR 
Recognise the implications of the hybrid way of working and assess how this can improve performance & commitment in your organisation
Interpret your organisation’s mindset, climate and conflict behaviour in order to optimise social dialogue in your organisation
Understand how digital strategy can be taken to the next level in HR with the use of AI

PRACTICAL INFO

To apply for this programme, you need to complete four steps:

1. Check your eligibility: Check if you meet the admissions criteria for the programme you want to apply for.

2. Submit your application: Fill out the application form and upload it together with your cv or LinkedIn profile.

3. Take part in an interview: When we receive your application, we’ll get in touch to set up an interview. This will take about an hour and will cover:

Your professional experience and current challenges
Your career ambitions
Why you want to take the programme
Which electives are right for you (if the programme includes electives)

4. Confirm your registration: If you pass the interview, we’ll send you a registration form. You’ll have two weeks to complete and submit the form to confirm your programme place.

PRACTICAL INFO

To apply for this programme, you need to complete four steps:

1. Check your eligibility: Check if you meet the admissions criteria for the programme you want to apply for.

2. Submit your application: Fill out the application form and upload it together with your cv or LinkedIn profile.

3. Take part in an interview: When we receive your application, we’ll get in touch to set up an interview. This will take about an hour and will cover:

Your professional experience and current challenges
Your career ambitions
Why you want to take the programme
Which electives are right for you (if the programme includes electives)

4. Confirm your registration: If you pass the interview, we’ll send you a registration form. You’ll have two weeks to complete and submit the form to confirm your programme place.
